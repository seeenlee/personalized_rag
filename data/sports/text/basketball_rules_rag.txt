[B1] A goal (field goal) is scored when a live ball enters the basket from above and remains in or passes through.
[B2] The goal line technically doesn't exist as a scoring boundary; the 'baseline' or 'end line' marks the court's edge.
[B3] A hand ball isn't a rule, but a 'kicked ball' violation occurs if a player intentionally strikes the ball with their foot.
[B4] The draft involves two rounds where teams select players from college or international leagues based on lottery odds.
[B5] Substitutions can be made whenever the ball is dead and the game clock is stopped.
[B6] A block occurs when a defender legally touches a field goal attempt while it is on its upward flight path.
[B7] A personal foul involves illegal contact with an opponent; six fouls result in a player being 'fouled out.'
[B8] A standard period (quarter) of play lasts 12 minutes in the NBA or 10 minutes in FIBA.
[B9] The captain is a designated leader who can address the referee during timeouts regarding game flow.
[B10] Out of bounds occurs when the ball or the player holding the ball touches the floor on or outside the boundary lines.
[B11] A technical foul is a non-contact foul for conduct, such as arguing or 'hanging on the rim,' resulting in one free throw.
[B12] A hat trick is not a basketball term; the equivalent is a 'Triple-Double' (double digits in three stat categories).
[B13] Overtime in the playoffs consists of 5-minute periods; these repeat indefinitely until a winner is determined.
[B14] The court dimensions are 94 feet long by 50 feet wide for professional play.
[B15] The defensive zone is often referred to as 'the paint' or the 'low post' where interior defense is prioritized.
[B16] Scoring from a distance is primarily done via the three-point shot from behind the arc.
[B17] A turnover happens when a player loses the ball via a steal, traveling, or an out-of-bounds violation.
[B18] Video review (Replay Center) checks clock issues, 'clear path' fouls, and whether a shot was a 2 or 3-pointer.
[B19] Unsportsmanlike conduct is called a 'Technical' or 'Flagrant' foul depending on the severity of the act.
[B20] A fast break is a transition play where the offense pushes the ball up-court before the defense can get set.
[B21] Legal contact is minimal; defenders must maintain a 'legal guarding position' and cannot use their hands to impede.
[B22] A double-header refers to two separate NBA games played at the same arena, often on a holiday.
[B23] A free shot (free throw) is an unhindered shot from the 15-foot line awarded after a shooting foul.
[B24] A shutout is impossible in basketball; the term is never used due to the high-scoring nature of the game.
[B25] A save is not an official stat, though 'saving a ball from going out of bounds' is a common hustle play.
