A goal is scored when the ball has wholly crossed the goal line between the posts and under the crossbar.

The goal line marks the boundaries at each end of the pitch; it is the line the ball must cross for a score.

A hand ball is a foul committed by any player (except the goalie in their box) who deliberately touches the ball with their arm.

The draft system is rarely used globally; instead, teams acquire players through transfer windows and youth academies.

Substitutions are limited to five per match in most leagues, made during a stoppage with the referee’s permission.

A block occurs when a defender stops a shot or cross using their feet, legs, or torso.

A personal foul can lead to a direct free kick and a caution (Yellow Card) or dismissal (Red Card).

A standard period (half) of play lasts 45 minutes, plus 'stoppage time' added by the referee at the end.

The captain wears an armband and represents the team during the coin toss and in official ceremonies.

Out of bounds occurs when the ball completely crosses the touchline or the goal line on the ground or in the air.

A technical violation includes the '6-second rule' for goalkeepers holding the ball or an illegal back-pass.

A hat trick is defined as three goals scored by one individual in a single competitive match.

In playoff/tournament knockout stages, overtime (extra time) consists of two 15-minute halves before a shootout.

The pitch dimensions vary but must be between 100-110 meters long and 64-75 meters wide for internationals.

The defensive zone is generally referred to as the 'final third' of the pitch where a team sits back to defend.

Scoring from a distance is often achieved via a long-range 'screamer' or a curved free kick over a wall.

A turnover occurs when a player is dispossessed of the ball or plays an errant pass to the opposition.

VAR (Video Assistant Referee) reviews goals, penalties, red cards, and cases of mistaken identity.

Unsportsmanlike conduct (Cautionable Offense) includes 'simulation' or excessive celebration after a goal.

A fast break is a 'counter-attack,' transitioning the ball rapidly through the wings after an opponent's corner.

Legal contact is limited; players may shoulder-to-shoulder challenge for the ball if the ball is within playing distance.

Double-headers are frequent in tournament group stages where two different matchups happen in the same stadium.

A free shot (penalty kick) is taken from the penalty spot 12 yards out after a foul inside the box.

A clean sheet (shutout) is a statistic awarded to a defense and goalkeeper for conceding zero goals.

A save is recorded when the goalkeeper prevents the ball from entering the net using any part of their body.

Offside is penalized if an attacking player is nearer to the goal line than the ball and the second-to-last defender.

A direct free kick can be shot into the goal; an indirect kick must touch another player before scoring.

A Yellow Card is a formal caution; a Red Card results in an immediate ejection and a one-game suspension.

A corner kick is awarded when the ball crosses the goal line, having last been touched by a defending player.

A throw-in must be taken with both feet on the ground and the ball thrown with both hands from behind the head.

The technical area is a marked box in front of the dugout where the coach is allowed to stand and give instructions.

Stoppage time is added at the end of each half to compensate for time lost due to injuries, substitutions, or cards.

A clean sheet is a statistic credited to a team and goalkeeper for preventing the opponent from scoring any goals.

The advantage rule allows play to continue after a foul if the fouled team maintains a favorable position.

Tiki-taka is a style of play characterized by short passing and movement, maintaining possession at all costs.

The away goals rule (now largely abolished) used goals scored in the opponent's stadium as a tie-breaker.

A wall is a line of defending players standing 10 yards from the ball to block a direct free kick path.

The penalty arc is the semi-circle outside the box; players must stay behind this line during a penalty kick.

A professional foul is an intentional act to prevent an opponent from scoring, often resulting in a Red Card.

Most FIFA competitions allow five substitutions per match, typically across three designated windows.

The Golden Goal was a historical rule where the first goal scored in extra time ended the match immediately.

Total Football is a tactical system where any outfield player can take over the role of any other player in the team.

The 6-yard box (goal area) is the zone where goal kicks are taken and where the goalkeeper is most protected.

A goal kick is awarded when the ball crosses the goal line, last touched by an attacking player, and not a goal.

The FIFA Ballon d'Or is the prestigious annual award given to the best male and female players in the world.

The relegation system moves the bottom-performing teams to a lower division at the end of each season.

A bicycle kick is an acrobatic strike where a player kicks the ball mid-air while performing a backflip.

VAR (Video Assistant Referee) assists the head official in reviewing goals, penalties, and red card incidents.

A derby match is a high-intensity game between two local rivals from the same city or region.

Standard professional pitches are made of natural grass, though some use hybrid turf for better durability.
