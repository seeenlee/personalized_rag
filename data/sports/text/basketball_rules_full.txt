A goal (field goal) is scored when a live ball enters the basket from above and remains in or passes through.

The goal line technically doesn't exist as a scoring boundary; the 'baseline' or 'end line' marks the court's edge.

A hand ball isn't a rule, but a 'kicked ball' violation occurs if a player intentionally strikes the ball with their foot.

The draft involves two rounds where teams select players from college or international leagues based on lottery odds.

Substitutions can be made whenever the ball is dead and the game clock is stopped.

A block occurs when a defender legally touches a field goal attempt while it is on its upward flight path.

A personal foul involves illegal contact with an opponent; six fouls result in a player being 'fouled out.'

A standard period (quarter) of play lasts 12 minutes in the NBA or 10 minutes in FIBA.

The captain is a designated leader who can address the referee during timeouts regarding game flow.

Out of bounds occurs when the ball or the player holding the ball touches the floor on or outside the boundary lines.

A technical foul is a non-contact foul for conduct, such as arguing or 'hanging on the rim,' resulting in one free throw.

A hat trick is not a basketball term; the equivalent is a 'Triple-Double' (double digits in three stat categories).

Overtime in the playoffs consists of 5-minute periods; these repeat indefinitely until a winner is determined.

The court dimensions are 94 feet long by 50 feet wide for professional play.

The defensive zone is often referred to as 'the paint' or the 'low post' where interior defense is prioritized.

Scoring from a distance is primarily done via the three-point shot from behind the arc.

A turnover happens when a player loses the ball via a steal, traveling, or an out-of-bounds violation.

Video review (Replay Center) checks clock issues, 'clear path' fouls, and whether a shot was a 2 or 3-pointer.

Unsportsmanlike conduct is called a 'Technical' or 'Flagrant' foul depending on the severity of the act.

A fast break is a transition play where the offense pushes the ball up-court before the defense can get set.

Legal contact is minimal; defenders must maintain a 'legal guarding position' and cannot use their hands to impede.

A double-header refers to two separate NBA games played at the same arena, often on a holiday.

A free shot (free throw) is an unhindered shot from the 15-foot line awarded after a shooting foul.

A shutout is impossible in basketball; the term is never used due to the high-scoring nature of the game.

A save is not an official stat, though 'saving a ball from going out of bounds' is a common hustle play.

A backcourt violation occurs when the offensive team moves the ball into the frontcourt and then back over the line.

Man-to-man defense assigns each player an opponent; zone defense assigns each player an area of the court.

The 24-second shot clock requires a team to attempt a field goal that hits the rim within that timeframe.

Traveling is taking more than two steps without dribbling; double dribbling is stopping and starting a dribble.

An 'And-1' occurs when a player is fouled while making a basket, earning one additional free throw attempt.

In the NBA, a player is disqualified after their 6th personal foul; in college, it is the 5th foul.

The restricted area is a 4-foot arc under the basket where defenders cannot draw a 'charging' foul.

A technical foul can be called on players or coaches for unsportsmanlike conduct or administrative errors.

Goaltending is touching the ball on its downward path to the rim; interference is touching the rim or net.

The pick and roll involves one offensive player setting a screen (pick) and then moving toward the basket (roll).

A triple-double is recorded when a player achieves double-digit totals in three of five statistical categories.

The 3-second rule prevents offensive players from remaining in the painted area for more than three seconds.

A clear path foul is an intentional foul when a player has no defenders between them and the basket on a break.

The Sixth Man award is given to the best performing player who primarily comes off the bench as a substitute.

A team enters the 'bonus' (one-and-one or two shots) once the opponent exceeds a set number of fouls per quarter.

The mid-court line rule requires the offense to advance the ball into the frontcourt within 8 or 10 seconds.

An alley-oop is a play where one player throws a pass near the basket for a teammate to catch and score mid-air.

Flopping is the act of exaggerating contact to deceive officials into calling a foul on the opponent.

Split free throws refer to a player making one of two attempts at the charity stripe.

Timeouts allow teams to advance the ball to half-court and reset the play during the final minutes of a game.

The Larry O'Brien Trophy is awarded annually to the team that wins the NBA Finals championship.

A fast break transition focuses on high-speed ball movement to score before the defense can recover.

A step-back jumper is a move where a player jumps backward away from the defender before shooting.

The Draft Lottery uses a weighted system to give the worst-performing teams a higher chance at the #1 pick.

The G-League is the official minor league organization of the NBA, used for player and coach development.
