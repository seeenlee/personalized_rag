A field goal is scored by kicking the ball over the crossbar and between the uprights of the opponent's goal.

The goal line is the front boundary of the end zone; crossing it with the ball results in a touchdown.

A hand ball is not a term; 'illegal touching' occurs if an ineligible receiver touches a forward pass.

The draft consists of seven rounds where teams select players from the collegiate level based on reverse-record order.

Substitutions are unlimited and can occur between any down, provided the players leave the field quickly.

A block is the legal use of the body to obstruct an opponent, usually to protect the quarterback or runner.

A personal foul (15-yard penalty) is called for dangerous acts like 'unnecessary roughness' or 'facemasking.'

A standard period (quarter) of play lasts 15 minutes; there are four quarters in a regulation game.

Teams appoint multiple captains (often 3-6) who meet at midfield for the coin toss and carry special patches.

Out of bounds occurs when a player or the ball touches any part of the sideline or end line.

A technical violation includes 'Illegal Formation' or 'Neutral Zone Infraction' before the snap.

A hat trick is not used; the equivalent might be a 'three-touchdown game' for a single player.

Playoff overtime allows both teams a possession to score unless the first team scores a touchdown immediately.

The field dimensions are 120 yards long (including end zones) and 53.3 yards wide.

The defensive zone is the 'secondary' or the 'red zone' (the area inside the 20-yard line).

Scoring from a distance occurs through long field goals (50+ yards) or deep 'Hail Mary' passing plays.

A turnover is a change of possession via an interception or a fumble recovery by the defense.

Video review (Coach's Challenge) allows for the reversal of calls like 'catch/no-catch' or 'ball spot.'

Unsportsmanlike conduct is a 'non-contact' foul, often for taunting or illegal demonstrations.

A fast break is not a term; the 'No-Huddle' or 'Hurry-Up' offense is the closest tactical equivalent.

Legal contact is extensive; blockers may use hands and arms within the frame of an opponent's body.

A double-header historically refers to Saturday college games followed by Sunday pro games.

A free shot (Free Kick) occurs after a safety, where the team must kick the ball to the opposition from their 20.

A shutout is when a defense holds the opposing offense to zero points for the entire 60 minutes.

A save is not a football term; a 'goal-line stand' is the term for a successful defensive stop at the net.

The line of scrimmage is an imaginary transverse line across the field that neither team may cross before the snap.

Teams have 4 'downs' to gain 10 yards; failing to do so results in a turnover or a punt to the opponent.

A safety occurs when an offensive player is tackled in their own end zone, awarding 2 points to the defense.

Pass interference is a penalty for impeding a receiver's ability to catch a ball before it arrives.

A touchback occurs when the ball is kicked into the end zone and the receiving team starts at their own 20 or 25-yard line.

The two-minute warning is a mandatory clock stoppage that occurs at the end of the 2nd and 4th quarters.

A coach's challenge involves throwing a red flag to signal a request for the officials to review a specific play.

A sack occurs when the defense tackles the quarterback behind the line of scrimmage before they can pass.

A neutral zone infraction is called when a defender moves into the neutral zone and causes an offensive player to flinch.

A Hail Mary is a very long, low-probability forward pass made at the end of a half in a desperate scoring attempt.

The Vince Lombardi Trophy is awarded annually to the winning team of the NFL Super Bowl.

Holding is a 10-yard penalty called when a player illegally grasps or hooks an opponent who doesn't have the ball.

An onside kick is a short kickoff intended for the kicking team to recover and maintain possession of the ball.

The Red Zone is the area of the field between the 20-yard line and the opponent's goal line.

Play action is a deceptive play where the quarterback fakes a handoff to a runner before attempting a pass.

A fair catch is signaled by a returner waving their arm, allowing them to catch a punt without being tackled.

Intentional grounding is a penalty called when a quarterback throws a pass away to avoid a sack with no receiver nearby.

A pick-six is a slang term for an interception that is returned by the defense for a touchdown.

The pocket is the protected area formed by the offensive line to give the quarterback time to throw.

Each team must have exactly 11 players on the field for every play; more or less results in a penalty.

Targeting is a penalty for initiating contact with the crown of the helmet or hitting a defenseless player in the head.

The salary cap is a limit on the total amount of money a professional team can spend on player contracts.

The NFL Scouting Combine is a week-long showcase where college prospects perform physical and mental tests.

A blitz is a defensive tactic where additional players beyond the defensive line rush the quarterback.

The Wildcat is a formation where a running back or receiver takes a direct snap instead of the quarterback.
