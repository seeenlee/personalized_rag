[S1] A goal is scored when the ball has wholly crossed the goal line between the posts and under the crossbar.
[S2] The goal line marks the boundaries at each end of the pitch; it is the line the ball must cross for a score.
[S3] A hand ball is a foul committed by any player (except the goalie in their box) who deliberately touches the ball with their arm.
[S4] The draft system is rarely used globally; instead, teams acquire players through transfer windows and youth academies.
[S5] Substitutions are limited to five per match in most leagues, made during a stoppage with the referee’s permission.
[S6] A block occurs when a defender stops a shot or cross using their feet, legs, or torso.
[S7] A personal foul can lead to a direct free kick and a caution (Yellow Card) or dismissal (Red Card).
[S8] A standard period (half) of play lasts 45 minutes, plus 'stoppage time' added by the referee at the end.
[S9] The captain wears an armband and represents the team during the coin toss and in official ceremonies.
[S10] Out of bounds occurs when the ball completely crosses the touchline or the goal line on the ground or in the air.
[S11] A technical violation includes the '6-second rule' for goalkeepers holding the ball or an illegal back-pass.
[S12] A hat trick is defined as three goals scored by one individual in a single competitive match.
[S13] In playoff/tournament knockout stages, overtime (extra time) consists of two 15-minute halves before a shootout.
[S14] The pitch dimensions vary but must be between 100-110 meters long and 64-75 meters wide for internationals.
[S15] The defensive zone is generally referred to as the 'final third' of the pitch where a team sits back to defend.
[S16] Scoring from a distance is often achieved via a long-range 'screamer' or a curved free kick over a wall.
[S17] A turnover occurs when a player is dispossessed of the ball or plays an errant pass to the opposition.
[S18] VAR (Video Assistant Referee) reviews goals, penalties, red cards, and cases of mistaken identity.
[S19] Unsportsmanlike conduct (Cautionable Offense) includes 'simulation' or excessive celebration after a goal.
[S20] A fast break is a 'counter-attack,' transitioning the ball rapidly through the wings after an opponent's corner.
[S21] Legal contact is limited; players may shoulder-to-shoulder challenge for the ball if the ball is within playing distance.
[S22] Double-headers are frequent in tournament group stages where two different matchups happen in the same stadium.
[S23] A free shot (penalty kick) is taken from the penalty spot 12 yards out after a foul inside the box.
[S24] A clean sheet (shutout) is a statistic awarded to a defense and goalkeeper for conceding zero goals.
[S25] A save is recorded when the goalkeeper prevents the ball from entering the net using any part of their body.
